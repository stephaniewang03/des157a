Visual Thinking Strategies Research

Posted on

05/09/2024

I think using photographs to learn close reading and visual thinking skills is very effective, since without context, anything can be true; stories can be made up just from looking at the imagery. In the article, the students look at the photographs and make their observations, and majority of the time they begin with the human subject in the main focus. It follows how stories are usually described, who, did what, when, where, etc. The most thought provoking photographs are usually ones with interactions within the picture, which can include two or more subjects, a subject that seems out of place, or a narrative that exists beyond the frame of the photo that is shown.

An example website that creatively showcases imagery and interaction is Guillaume Colombel. The site uses dynamic typography, videos and captions that tells a narrative as you scroll down, encouraging user interaction.
