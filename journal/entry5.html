Game Design Research

Posted on

11/14/2024

Amir Dori's article on "Game Design UX Best Practices" shares smart, practical tips for mobile game design that really make you think about how users interact with games. As a UX design student, I found it fascinating how simple tweaks, like placing the “Store” button in easy-to-reach spots around the bottom corners of the screen, can boost engagement.
Making sure sliders show a bit of off-screen content also encourages users to explore further, while replacing the X button (close) on pop-ups for cancel or done, would make people actually read the info instead of instantly closing it.
Another tip that really caught my attention is the idea of placing user decisions that benefit the game, like a "Confirm Purchase" button, on the right side of pop-ups. Users naturally look and tap on the right side of the screen especially for right handed users, so positioning these options there increases the chances they'll select them.
