Research Form Design

Posted on

10/15/2024

The article lists several key principles for designing user-friendly forms. First, it talks about minimizing input fields, reducing cognitive load, and improving completion rates. Second, it recommends a single-column layout to maintain a natural flow and avoid distractions. Third, it suggests breaking down long forms into multiple steps to reduce abandonment. The article also emphasizes clear labeling and corresponding field sizing. Other best practices include minimizing dropdown menus, ensuring users can navigate the fields with tab keys, providing matching keyboards, etc. Finally, the article highlights the significance of accessibility in form design. By following these principles, product designers can create forms that are not only visually appealing but also intuitive and efficient, ultimately leading to higher conversion rates. (Link to Article)

I think a website that demonstrates this is Form Smarts. The form has clear labels with each near its corresponding field with right alignment. The field size also matches the input type, and required fields are marked with red asteriks.
